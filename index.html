Discover Sepraxa

Why Sepraxa?
Sepraxa (seproxatil) is the first-in-class oral TLR4 inhibitor for sepsis, offering targeted anti-inflammatory action without immunosuppression. Designed for critically ill patients, it’s a safe, precise, and oral breakthrough in sepsis care.
Clinical Highlights
Sepraxa reduced 28-day mortality by 20% in the Phase III PULSE-3 trial. It improved SOFA scores (Δ = -6), shortened ICU stays by 3 days, and reduced overall hospital time. Effective against both gram-negative and gram-positive infections.
How to Administer
Sepraxa is given as a 150 mg oral tablet once daily for 7 days. It has no food restrictions, with proven bioequivalence in both fasted and fed states. With no IV required, it's ideal for ICU, step-down, and emergency settings.

What Makes Sepraxa Stand Out

No immunosuppression: Normal WBC and neutrophil counts maintained
Low adverse event rate (< 2%), mostly mild (e.g., nausea)
No liver or renal toxicity; ALT <40 U/L in sterile sepsis models
